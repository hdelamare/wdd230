Lightning Over the Lake

by Wiliam Writer

As storms rolled in over South Eastern Idaho this past week, a local photographer from Fish Haven was able to snag this beautiful shot of lightning striking over Bear Lake. We here at Sunny Day News thought that this would be a great example to remind the residents of Idaho on the dangers that thunderstorms can present. Remember the 30-30 rule! That if 30 seconds or less have passed from the time of seeing lighting and hearing the thunder, that the storm is close enough to you to be dangerous. Also, remember to wait at least 30 minutes from the last lightning flash to leave shelter. If you're caught in a thunderstorm, a building or car are the safest places to be. Stay away from concrete surfaces and avoid taking showers or baths as lightning can travel pipes and wires in your home. If you find yourself in a situation where you can't seek shelter or are in an open area, immediately get off elevated ground and seek low ground. Avoid large bodies of water and isolated trees. If all else fails, crouch down into a ball with your head between your knees and cover your ears with your hands. DO NOT lie flat on the ground! Try to limit your contact with the ground as much as possible. If you're out on open water, retreat inside your cabin and wait the storm out. If your boat doesn't have a cabin, either try to get ashore and find shelter or stay as low as you can in the boat. That's going to do it for this week's weather tip.
