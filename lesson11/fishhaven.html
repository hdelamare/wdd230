Bear Witness of the Bears

by Jennifer Journalist

Yesterday, a local hiker and photographer from the Bear Lake area found himself fortunate (or unfortunate...) to stumble across a pair of grizzly bears near Fish Haven. He was able to get this great shot of the two bears before quickly making his way out of the area. Bears are amazing creatures, but can be quite dangerous too if you happen to encounter one out in the wild and don't know what to do. Because we live in bear country, we here at Sunny Day News thought we'd take this opportunity to remind our community of how to stay safe while hiking the many beautiful trails in our area of Idaho. If you're planning a hike soon, make sure you avoid hiking alone and stick to the trail. It's also best to try to hike during daylight hours, as bears are most active in the early morning to late evening hours of the day. Keep any food you're bringing tightly packed and avoid wearing scented perfumes/colognes or lotions as forgetting any of this could easily attract a bear. Lastly, make sure to stick together and make noise as you're hiking, this way any animals in the area will hear you coming and won't be startled by your presence and can leave the area. We here at Sunny Day News hope our community is making the best of this beautiful summer weather and getting outside to enjoy nature! Remember, if you get any great shots of anything you see out there to post them to our Gallery here on our website! Thanks for reading, and as always, be sure to follow us on social media, or check back here for the best news coverage of your community.
