Go, Geyser! Go!

by Robert Reporter

This past weekend, residents from Soda Springs, Idaho gathered at Geyser Park located behind the historic Enders Hotel for a town celebration. The reason for the special occasion was to celebrate the Geyser Park's geyser's 750,000th eruption which has been happening every hour on the hour since the geyser was founded all the way back in 1937. The event took place at the 7pm hour mark this past Sunday and was preceded by a special presentation and time capsule ceremony by the town's mayor that included a brief history of the town and how the geyser at Geyser Park came to be. Residents of the town then placed their items into the time capsule and had it buried to be dug up in about another 30 years in order to celebrate the geyser's 1,000,000th eruption. If you happened to miss this historic event for the town, be sure to come back in 30 years for the next celebration event, or feel free to check out Geyser Park anytime. The park is located at 39 W, 1st S in Soda Springs where the geyser flow is controlled by the city and is set to go off every hour on the hour.
